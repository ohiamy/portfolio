    Velox Platform · Enterprise SaaS · 2022

    Access for All: Leading a WCAG 2.1 Compliance Initiative

    A major enterprise contract was at risk after Velox failed an accessibility audit with 200+ violations. I led the cross-functional remediation program — achieving WCAG 2.1 AA certification in 4 months and retaining $2.4M ARR.

    My Role
    Program Lead, UX Designer
    Timeline
    4 months (Q3–Q4 2022)
    Team
    2 Designers, 6 Engineers, QA Lead
    Tools
    Figma, axe, NVDA, VoiceOver, JAWS
    Overview

    A contract at risk

    Velox Platform's largest enterprise customer — a federal agency — had a contractual requirement for WCAG 2.1 AA compliance. A third-party audit flagged 214 accessibility violations across the platform. The customer gave Velox 4 months to remediate or the contract would not renew — representing $2.4M ARR.

    Leadership asked me to lead the program. I had 4 months, a team of 2 junior designers, 6 engineers, and a QA lead who had never run an accessibility testing program.

    214Accessibility violations identified in the initial audit
    $2.4MARR at risk from contract non-renewal
    4 monthsTime to achieve WCAG 2.1 AA certification
    Process

    Triage, remediate, prevent

    I structured the program in three phases. The first two weeks were spent on triage: categorizing violations by severity (blocker vs. high vs. medium), estimating engineering effort, and prioritizing the 47 blockers that would prevent screen reader users from completing core workflows.

    Weeks 3–14 were the remediation sprint. I led weekly design reviews and daily engineering standups. I created a reusable accessible patterns library — 28 components with ARIA patterns, keyboard behavior specs, and screen reader test scripts — so engineers had clear, tested implementation guidance rather than interpreting WCAG criteria on their own.

    Weeks 15–16 were validation: a full re-audit using axe, a manual screen reader pass (NVDA + Chrome, VoiceOver + Safari, JAWS + Edge), and a session with two users who are blind and use screen readers professionally.

    "I've worked on remediation projects before where the design team handed us a list and disappeared. Amy was in every standup, unblocking us in real time. That's why we hit the deadline."

    — Lead Engineer, Velox Platform

    Outcomes

    Results and lasting impact

    ✓ AAWCAG 2.1 AA certification achieved on schedule
    $2.4MARR contract retained
    0New accessibility violations in the 18 months post-launch

    Beyond the certification, the accessible patterns library was integrated into the product's design system — making compliance a baseline going forward rather than a remediation project. We also introduced an accessibility gate in the design review process: every new component must pass axe and a manual keyboard test before it ships.

    This project changed how I think about accessibility. It's not a compliance layer bolted on at the end — it's an indicator of the overall quality of the product's interaction design. Every accessibility fix we made also improved the experience for users without disabilities.
